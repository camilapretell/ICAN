About us

Expanding Access to Global Higher Education

We are a global coalition of trusted, high-quality college access organizations that identify and prepare talented students from low-resource backgrounds to study at universities outside of their home country. Through this mission, we expand access and improve outcomes for high-achieving, low-income international students.

🌐

Shared best practices

We facilitate peer learning on student identification, selection, advising, preparation, student success, and ethical engagement with universities.

🛡️

Strategic coordination

We serve as an organized point of engagement for universities recruiting talented, low‑income international students from abroad.

🤝

Coordinated Action

We increase transparency, trust, and efficiency in international student recruitment and support.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

ICAN membership is open to non-profit or mission-driven organizations that identify and support high-achieving students from low-resource backgrounds to access undergraduate education outside their home country. Eligible organizations must run a competitive selection process, provide college access advising or mentorship, and demonstrate a commitment to ethical engagement and student success.

Yes. ICAN offers three membership types:

  • Executive Members: Founding organizations invited to co-create ICAN's structure, strategy, and standards.
  • General Members: Organizations admitted through a formal application and review process after ICAN's launch.
  • Mentee Organizations: Early-stage or developing organizations that receive support from ICAN and undergo a two-year trial period before potential admission as General Members.

Organizations can join ICAN through a structured process that includes nomination and a formal application. Applicants must provide details about their mission, selection and advising model, team, and student outcomes. Membership decisions are based on alignment with ICAN's values, quality of programming, and capacity to contribute to the network. Promising early-stage organizations may first enter as Mentee Organizations.

No. ICAN does not provide scholarships or direct financial support. Instead, it strengthens the ecosystem of organizations that help students access funding and educational opportunities.

No. ICAN is not a centralized application platform or placement service. Member organizations independently support their students through the college application process.
